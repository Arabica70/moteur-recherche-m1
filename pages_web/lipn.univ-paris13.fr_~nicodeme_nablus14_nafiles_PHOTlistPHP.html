Photos of the trip to Jerusalem of Pierre, Brigitte, Nicolas, Cecile, Cyril and Sarah

We met in Jerusalem
  • Jehudit Elkana, an Israeli activist who founded with two other women Wachsom Watch to try to protect the Palestinians on the checkpoints; she is presently member of Yesh Din, an organisation that sues (with little success) the settlers and the soldiers after acts of violence against Palestinians. Her husband was a famous philosoph of science and a miraculous survivor of Auschwitz. Jehudit told us that the attack of Israel on Gaza during July-August 2014 was fully deliberate.
  • Ali Jedda, a black Palestinian and citizen of the old city of Jerusalem. Ali's father was a Muslim from Senegal; he went to Jerusalem as a pilgrim and stayed there. During the 80s, the Israelis made a massacre somewhere, and as a revenge, Ali organized some kind of bombing where two Israelis got injured; he was caught and condemned to 17 years of prison. He refused later to work for the Palestinian Authority to keep his freedom. He got children and says that presently he would not do any bombing again, in particular considering the risk of killing or injuring innocents. Ali works by leading politic guided tours of the old city of Jerusalem. He made us remark in the Arab quarter that a provocative settler was accompanied by two armed body-guards.
